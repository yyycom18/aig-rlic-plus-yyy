## [NEW COMPONENT – Indicator Evaluation Framework]
> The Indicator Evaluation Framework contextualizes indicators by pairing their economic identity and main/secondary use-cases (generated by the research agent) with quantitative evidence and strategy scores, providing richer first-glance understanding on the dashboard.

Research is the authoritative source for indicator definition, economic interpretation, and use-case classification used in the Indicator Identity Layer.

**The research agent defines the conceptual meaning of each indicator, its economic role, and its primary and secondary use-cases. These definitions guide how indicators are contextualized within the evaluation framework.**

**Research Agent Responsibilities**:
- Define and maintain the indicator use-case taxonomy and label mapping referenced in the Identity Layer (`data/indicator_taxonomy.json`, `data/indicator_use_cases.json`).
- Provide plain-language summary, context, and motivation for each indicator and their associated use-cases (for display above the radars).
- Ensure all narrative content used in the Apps overview aligns with both the economic literature and ongoing research briefs.

No change is required to research workflow; this evaluation layer serves as a narrative-driven consumer of research-side outputs.
